Russet Potatoes

Planted: Spring 2025
Container: Grow bags (standard, not bottomless yet)
Status: Actively growing – vines are lush with room to expand

Why We Grow It

Russets are our base crop—dense, versatile, and deeply grounding. They're our filler, our fallback, and our comfort. In this heat-blasted soil, they root deep and give back slow.

How We Grow It

  • We planted sprouting store-bought russets directly in compost-heavy soil.
  • Using standard grow bags for now—bottomless containers planned for later.
  • As vines grew, we hilled up around the stems using mulch, compost, and loose dirt.
  • Watering is done early morning—deep but infrequent to mimic desert rain logic.
  • We’ll harvest when the vines yellow and collapse, then let the soil rest and breathe.

Spiral Reflections

This crop teaches patience. It stays hidden while others rise fast. But it’s working—quietly, underground. Like trauma healing. Like root systems building strength before fruit. It doesn’t ask for attention—it just needs space, time, and trust. And so do we.
