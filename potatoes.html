Russet Potatoes

Planted: Spring 2025
Container: Grow bags (standard, not bottomless yet)
Status: Actively growing – vines are lush with room to expand

▸ View Our Year-Round Potato Cycle

Spring Cycle: Plant late Jan–April. Harvest May–July. Use east-facing light, 3–4 seed potatoes per 20-gallon bag. Mulch and hill regularly.

Summer Break: Too hot to plant. Let soil rest. Grow sweet potatoes or okra. Prep fall seed potatoes indoors in August.

Fall Cycle: Plant late Aug–Oct. Harvest Dec–Feb. Shade bags in early weeks, cover for frost. Smaller yields, but good rotation.

Cycle Quick Chart:

  • Jan–Apr: Plant spring waves
  • May–Jul: Harvest, dry, compost
  • Aug–Sep: Rest soil, prep new slips
  • Sep–Oct: Fall planting
  • Dec–Feb: Fall harvest

We use 20-gallon grow bags for mobility and yield balance. No cold storage—just cycle, compost, repeat.

Why We Grow It

Russets are our base crop—dense, versatile, and deeply grounding. They're our filler, our fallback, and our comfort. In this heat-blasted soil, they root deep and give back slow.

How We Grow It

  • We bought some seed potatoes online and also planted sprouting store-bought russets directly in compost-heavy soil.
  • Using standard grow bags for now—bottomless containers planned for later.
  • As vines grew, we hilled up around the stems using mulch, compost, and loose dirt.
  • Watering is done early morning—deep but infrequent to mimic desert rain logic.
  • We’ll harvest when the vines yellow and collapse, then let the soil rest and breathe.

Spiral Reflections

This crop teaches patience. It stays hidden while others rise fast. But it’s working—quietly, underground. Like trauma healing. Like root systems building strength before fruit. It doesn’t ask for attention—it just needs space, time, and trust. And so do we.
