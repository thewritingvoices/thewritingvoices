Many people seeking healing from trauma find themselves caught between two inadequate options: traditional therapy that may not address their specific needs, or self-help resources that lack the depth and safety required for trauma work. The Fort Workbook represents a third path.

Free Crisis Resources

These specialized adaptations of The Fort methodology are provided free of charge to populations experiencing ongoing crisis, demonstrating that trauma recovery principles can serve urgent humanitarian needs while maintaining clinical effectiveness.

Our Humanitarian Commitment

We believe that access to effective trauma recovery resources should not depend on economic circumstances or political stability. These crisis adaptations represent our commitment to serving populations whose healing needs transcend commercial frameworks. By providing specialized resources during active emergencies, we honor the principle that trauma-informed care must be available when people need it most, not only when they can afford it.

What Makes This Different

The Fort Workbook utilizes the SpiralLogic‚Ñ¢ methodology, a framework that recognizes trauma recovery as a non-linear process requiring multiple approaches working in harmony. Rather than forcing healing into artificial timelines or rigid steps, this approach adapts to how trauma actually affects the mind, body, and spirit. The workbook integrates Internal Family Systems concepts with practical daily life management, creating a holistic foundation for sustainable recovery.

The methodology addresses a critical gap in available resources. While traditional therapy provides valuable professional support, many trauma survivors need practical tools they can use independently between sessions or in situations where therapy access is limited. The Fort Workbook provides this structured self-guided approach while maintaining the safety protocols essential for trauma work.

Who This Serves

This resource serves adult trauma survivors who are ready to take an active role in their healing process. It particularly benefits individuals dealing with complex trauma, those who have found traditional approaches insufficient, and people seeking alternatives that honor their autonomy while providing comprehensive support. The workbook recognizes that healing happens within the context of daily life and provides tools for managing both therapeutic work and practical life challenges.

The approach acknowledges that trauma affects every aspect of life‚Äîfrom decision-making and relationships to basic daily functioning. Rather than compartmentalizing healing work, The Fort Workbook integrates recovery principles into a comprehensive life management system that supports sustainable progress.
